Thit Kho Tau

Thit Kho Tau is a popular dish in South Vietnam. This specialty dish is often made to use during Tet because it can be processed and left for a long time, so when you eat rice you can warm it up to eat right away with rice without having to waste time cooking on Tet. .

In the North, this dish is cooked without coconut water and boiled eggs. The water used to store meat and eggs is coconut water. Pork is usually bacon, or meat with both lean and fat. Meat cut into large square pieces, eggs are boiled, peeled and then put in storage with meat. Spices used include: pepper, fish sauce, chili, sugar, and some other spices. This medium-flooded meats-egg-coconut mixture is simmered over low heat until tender. Duck seed braised meat can be served with rice and pickles. Instead of duck eggs, eggs are also used in ship storage.

This dish is also often found in popular restaurants because it is easy to make, cheap and delicious. In some central and southern provinces, duck meat is added with bamboo shoots (not young bamboo shoots) to cook it into: Braised duck with bamboo shoots.
